Inferential Statistics: Stroop Task¶

Background Information¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the inkin which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent wordscondition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent wordscondition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

This task will show the null hypothesis designed such that it will be rejected if there is a significant difference in the mean response time of each sample population for the two conditions under investigation. Random variation explains observable differences in this model.

Represented mathematically as follows:

H0: C = I

Where:

H0 = Null hypothesis.
C = The population mean response time under the congruent condition.
I = The population mean response time under the incongruent condition.

At initial review the mean for each condition appears fairly different, 14.05 seconds for the congruent condition and 22.02 seconds for the incongruent condition. We will seek to explore the nature of the difference to determine whether chance is the contributing factor or that the results arise from a statistically significant difference between the sample populations. We begin thinking the results are due to random variance and will reject this assumption in the null hypothesis to demonstrate a statistically significant difference occurring in the alternate hypothesis.

The alternate hypothesis is designed to be true if the null hypothesis is rejected. This hypothesis will show the population mean for the response time under congruent condition as significantly less than the population mean for the response time under incongruent condition.

Represented mathematically as follows:

Ha: C < I

Where:

Ha = Alternate hypothesis.
C = The population mean response time under the congruent condition.
I = The population mean response time under the incongruent condition.

In initial review of the data set, the reason we include the less-than symbol in the alternate hypothesis is because there is a good chance the congruent condition response times could be significantly less than the incongruent condition response times. This is a directional, one-tailed test. If we were unable to make a distinction one way or the other we could use a different alternate hypothesis requiring the use of a two-tailed non-directional test. The alternate hypothesis for this example would be represented as Ha: C I.

A t-test will be performed on this data instead of a z-test because we are comparing sample means instead of the comparison of population mean to sample mean. We also have no additional details regarding our population parameters. A t-test is also suitable for our investigation given the small sample size and the potential for non-normality in the data distribution.

5. Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?¶

With the consideration that the two treatments, congruent and incongruent conditions, are measured for the same sample population a paired-sample t-test will be used here. We will test whether or not the means for the pairs of observation are significantly different. We will use a confidence interval of 90% and our Degrees of Freedom = 23 (df = n-1). The critical t value is 1.714.

We will determine whether or not the null hypothesis can rejected (if there is a statistical significance between the two conditions) by confirming that the calculated test statistic is greater than the critical t value. The t distribution table will help determine the critical t-value: https://s3.amazonaws.com/udacity-hosted-downloads/t-table.jpg

6. Optional: What do you think is responsible for the effects observed? Can you think of an alternative or similar task that would result in a similar effect? Some research about the problem will be helpful for thinking about these two questions!¶

The observed effects can possibly be attributed to the additional cognitive load involved in processing the information, disregarding the independent word in order to focus on and say aloud the text color. In relation to the distractions that text color can produce, it is standard practice in many publishing style guides to maintain consistent text color (usually in black) throughout a piece of communication in order to facilitate the ease of reading. This test focuses on the color of the text instead of the word which seems to complicate the normal reading process further. If the participant is forced to read and consider, then disregard the meaning of the word and say the text color aloud, it is understandable that this incongruent condition will result in longer processing and essentially completion times.

The potential for exploration on alternative tasks is what has kept this particular test common since it's publication in English in 1935. I think it would be interesting to explore the results among different age demographics performing the task. We could refine further by expanding the population to various countries.
